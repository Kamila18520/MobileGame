The first modern Summer Olympic Games were held in 1896 in Athens, Greece.
The first Olympic Games for women were held in 1900 in Paris, France, although they were limited to certain sports.
The first opening ceremony involving athletes from different countries took place during the 1908 Olympic Games in London.
In 1936, during the Berlin Olympic Games, Jesse Owens, an African-American track and field athlete, won 4 gold medals, defeating Nazi propaganda about racial superiority.
The Olympic Games were canceled in 1916, 1940, and 1944 due to World War I and II.
In 1960, the Summer Olympic Games were held in Rome, Italy, and it was the first time the event was televised worldwide.
In 1980, at the Moscow Olympic Games, the United States and several other countries boycotted the event in protest against the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan.
The Olympic Games have 5 core sports: athletics, gymnastics, cycling, swimming, and wrestling.
In 2008, in Beijing, China, Michael Phelps won 8 gold medals in swimming, the highest number of gold medals won at a single Games.
In 2020, the Summer Olympic Games were postponed from 2020 to 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
The first Winter Olympic Games were held in 1924 in Chamonix, France.
The first winter Olympic sport added to the program was ice hockey, which debuted at the 1924 Winter Olympic Games.
In 1980, at the Lake Placid Winter Olympic Games in New York, USA, the USA hockey team defeated the USSR team in one of the most famous games in the sport's history.
In 1928, at the St. Moritz Winter Olympic Games in Switzerland, Canada won a gold medal in ice hockey, but it was later stripped because it violated amateur status rules.
In 1988, at the Calgary Winter Olympic Games in Canada, Eddie "The Eagle" Edwards, a British skier, gained popularity as the worst ski jumper in Olympic history.
At the 1994 Winter Olympic Games in Lillehammer, Norway, snowboarding debuted as an Olympic discipline.
In 1992, at the Albertville Winter Olympic Games in France, freestyle skiing debuted.
In 1998, at the Nagano Winter Olympic Games in Japan, snowboarding debuted as an official Olympic discipline.
In 2002, at the Salt Lake City Winter Olympic Games in the USA, skeleton debuted as a discipline.
The first Winter Olympic Games in Asia were held in 2018 in Pyeongchang, South Korea.
The symbolic Olympic flame lighting the torch at the games is a flame that ignites during the opening ceremony and extinguishes during the closing ceremony.
The Olympic anthem was first used at the opening ceremony of the 1896 games.
When Greece hosted the 2004 Olympic Games, they revived the tradition of the ancient Olympic Games, including using the Panathinaiko Stadium.
The word "Olympiad" comes from the ancient Greek name "Olympion," meaning Olympic sports competition.
During the 1900 games in Paris, women competed in gymnastics, tennis, sailing, and croquet.
In 1912, Jim Thorpe, a Native American athlete, won gold medals in the decathlon and pentathlon at the Stockholm Olympic Games.
The 1936 Berlin Olympic Games were the first to be televised.
